DMCA Policy

Digital Millennium Copyright Act Notice and Takedown Procedures

1. DMCA Notice Requirements

To file a DMCA takedown notice, please provide the following information:

  • A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized agent
  • Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed
  • Identification of the infringing material and its location
  • Your contact information (address, telephone number, email)
  • A statement of good faith belief that the use is not authorized
  • A statement that the information is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner

2. Counter-Notice Procedures

If you believe your content was wrongly removed, you may submit a counter-notice containing:

  • Your physical or electronic signature
  • Identification of the removed material and its previous location
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was wrongly removed
  • Your contact information and consent to local federal court jurisdiction

3. Filing Process

Follow these steps to submit a DMCA notice:

  1. Prepare your notice with all required information
  2. Send the notice to our designated copyright agent
  3. Allow up to 2-3 business days for initial response
  4. Provide additional information if requested

4. Our Response Process

Upon receiving a valid DMCA notice, we will:

  • Remove or disable access to the claimed infringing material
  • Notify the affected user of the takedown
  • Document the takedown in our records
  • Take appropriate action for repeat infringers

5. Repeat Infringer Policy

We maintain a strict policy for repeat infringers:

  • First violation: Warning and content removal
  • Second violation: Temporary account suspension
  • Third violation: Permanent account termination
