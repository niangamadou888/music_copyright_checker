How To Check If A Song Is Copyrighted?

There are several methods to check if a specific song is copyrighted or not. Here are the three most prominent ways:

Use YouTube Content ID

One of the simplest methods to check whether an audio track is safe or not is to post it. Yes, that’s right! You can upload your video with the song as a private or unlisted video. As soon as the uploading is completed, YouTube will run its automated Content ID checks against its database of copyrighted tracks. The simplest step you can take when using external music is to check the descriptions and details of the video. Many artists include copyright information in the descriptions of their music videos on YouTube. Some may include it either at the beginning or end of the video with a ‘©’ symbol. Best Practices For Using Music Legally

Music can bring life to your video. But it's important to do it the right way to avoid copyright issues. Here are some best practices to ensure you're using music legally:

Check If A Song is Copyrighted

Before using any music– no matter how short it is – check if it is copyrighted. Never assume that a song isn’t copyrighted just because it’s popular or widely shared. First of all, review the descriptions and video to know if there’s any copyright marks. Then, check using our YouTube audio copyright checker or through the YouTube content ID system directly. It’s not that time-consuming, considering the consequences of copyright infringement.

Use Royalty-Free Music

The most practical approach to staying safe from copyright issues is the usage of royalty-free music. These tracks come with licenses that allow you to use the music without the risk of copyright claims. You have two options:
  1. Royalty-free music: You just need to pay a one-time fee to use the music safely in your content. YouTube gives you the option to buy a license for music from their platform.
  2. Copyright-free YouTube music: As the name suggests, it refers to music tracks that are not protected by copyright law. You can use them freely as much as you want.
Platforms like the YouTube Audio Library offer a large selection of copyright-free and royalty-free tracks that are safe to use on the platform.

Read The License Agreement Carefully

A royalty-free or Creative Commons License doesn’t mean that you can sit back and relax. Some licenses may have specific requirements, such as giving credit to the artist. Here are the key points to look at in a license agreement:

  1. Attribution requirements: Some songs may require you to give credit to the artist in the video description.
  2. Commercial use: Ensure the license allows you to use the music for commercial purposes if you're monetizing your videos.
  3. Modifications: Check if you're allowed to remix or modify the track in any way.

For extra safety, always keep a copy of the license agreement or proof of purchase, especially if you’re using royalty-free music. This can protect you if a claim is ever made.

Use Licensed Music For Commercial Usage

The fact is that most royalty-free tracks won’t allow commercial usage. You may need to purchase a license for it. Even though it is expensive, obtaining the copyright holder’s permission ensures that you are safe.

Some websites offer easy licensing for commercial tracks, such as AudioJungle. They simplify the process of acquiring legal rights to popular songs.

Avoid Common Copyright Pitfalls

There are several misconceptions that can lead content creators into legal trouble. Here are some things to avoid:

  1. Do not rely on short clips: Many creators think that using just a few seconds of a song won’t trigger copyright claims. However, YouTube’s Content ID system is highly advanced and can detect even short snippets of copyrighted music.
  2. Don’t assume ‘fair use’ applies: Fair use is a legal defense that only applies in specific cases, such as commentary, criticism, or parody. Just because you’re using a song for educational purposes doesn’t mean you’re protected.
  3. Be cautious with remixes and covers: Even if you’re adding your own creativity to a song for a remix, it’s still based on copyrighted material. You may need to obtain permission from the original artist.

Stay Updated

Copyright laws change from time to time. So, you must stay informed about the current regulation, both legally and from the side of platforms like YouTube.
